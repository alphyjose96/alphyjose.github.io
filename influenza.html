Influenza Mortality Analysis

Explored influenza deaths by season, geography, and age group to support healthcare planning and resource allocation.

Business Problem

Healthcare planners need to understand when and where influenza mortality is highest in order to allocate staff and resources more effectively.

Key Objectives

  • Identify peak influenza seasons
  • Compare mortality across age groups
  • Map high-risk states
  • Support planning with clear visual insights

Data Source

Influenza mortality data combined with U.S. population data for per-capita analysis.

Tools

Python, pandas, Tableau, Excel.

Analytical Process

I cleaned mortality and population datasets, standardized dates and state identifiers, and grouped records by month, state, and age group. I then calculated deaths per capita, summarized seasonal patterns, and created charts and maps to highlight high-risk periods and populations.

Insights

  • Influenza deaths peak during winter months.
  • Seniors aged 65+ face much higher mortality risk.
  • Certain states show disproportionately high death rates per capita.

Recommendations

  • Increase staffing ahead of winter surge periods.
  • Prioritize vulnerable populations in outreach and care planning.
  • Use regional targeting for resource allocation.
