Market Analysis of Video Game Sales to Support Product Strategy at GameCo

Business Problem

GameCo, a video game company, wanted to use historical sales data to understand market trends, customer preferences, and competitor performance. The goal is to support strategic decisions around game development, marketing investment, and regional sales focus.

Key Objectives

  • Identify the most popular genres by region
  • Analyze how market leadership changed over time
  • Compare platform preferences across regions
  • Support marketing allocation with data-driven insights

Data Source

VGChartz — historical video game sales (1980– 2016).

Tools

Excel (Pivot Tables, descriptive statistics, charts).

Analytical Process

I cleaned the dataset by addressing missing values, removing duplicates, standardizing labels, and preparing the data for pivot-table analysis. I then grouped and aggregated sales across regions, genres, and platforms to identify patterns over time and translated the results into stakeholder-friendly visuals.

Insights

  • Europe emerged as a stronger growth market in later years.
  • Action, Shooter, and Sports were dominant in Western markets.
  • Japan showed stronger preference for role-playing and handheld gaming.
  • Platform adoption varies sharply by region, with consoles strong in the West and handheld gaming dominant in Japan

Recommendations

  • Increase marketing investment in Europe.
  • Prioritize Action, Shooter, and Sports titles for North America and Europe.
  • Develop region-specific platform strategies, especially for Japan.
