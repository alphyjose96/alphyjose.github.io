Instacart Grocery Basket Analysis

Explored customer ordering behavior, spending patterns, and department demand to support targeted marketing and segmentation strategy.

Business Problem

Instacart needed to better understand differences in customer behavior so that marketing efforts could be targeted instead of using a one-size-fits-all approach.

Key Objectives

  • Identify busiest days and hours for orders
  • Understand spending patterns by time and region
  • Measure popularity across product departments
  • Compare ordering behavior across customer segments

Data Source

Instacart transactional datasets combined with customer demographic data and product metadata.

Tools

Python, pandas, NumPy, matplotlib, seaborn, Jupyter Notebook.

Analytical Process

I cleaned and merged multiple Instacart datasets, corrected data types, handled missing values, and created derived variables such as loyalty flags, spending categories, and customer profiles. I then used groupby aggregations and Python visualizations to analyze order timing, department demand, and behavioral differences across regions and segments.

Insights

  • Orders peak on weekends and during late morning to afternoon hours.
  • Produce and Dairy/Eggs dominate department demand.
  • Customer value and spending patterns vary across regions.

Recommendations

  • Run campaigns during lower-demand periods to smooth volume.
  • Prioritize inventory and promotions for top departments.
  • Use regional and customer-level segmentation for targeted marketing.
